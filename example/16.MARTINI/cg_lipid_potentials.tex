\section{Coarse-Grained Lipid Model for Hybrid All-Atom / Dry MARTINI Simulations}
% ==============================================================================

\subsection{Single-Particle CG Lipid Representation}

Each DOPC lipid, comprising 14 MARTINI beads in the dry MARTINI force field~\cite{MARTINI},
is coarse-grained to a single oriented particle carrying a 6D state vector
$(\mathbf{r}, \hat{\mathbf{n}})$, where $\mathbf{r} \in \mathbb{R}^3$ is the
center-of-mass position of the 14-bead lipid and $\hat{\mathbf{n}} \in S^2$ is a
unit direction vector representing the mean lipid orientation (from the lipid
tail toward the head group). The orientation vector is anchored by a hidden
orientation site placed at $\mathbf{r} + \ell\,\hat{\mathbf{n}}$ with
$\ell = 5.0\,\text{\AA}$, connected to the CG particle by a stiff harmonic bond
$U_{\text{bond}} = \frac{1}{2}k_b(r - \ell)^2$ with
$k_b = 20.0\,E_{\text{up}}/\text{\AA}^2$.

The CG particle mass $M_{\text{CG}} = 1008.0\,\text{g/mol}$ is the sum of the
14 DOPC bead masses; the orientation site mass accounts for the remainder such
that the total DOPC mass is preserved.

\subsection{Angle Convention}

All orientation-dependent potentials use the same angle convention.  For a pair
of interacting oriented particles with positions $\mathbf{r}_1, \mathbf{r}_2$
and unit direction vectors $\hat{\mathbf{n}}_1, \hat{\mathbf{n}}_2$, define
the inter-particle unit vector $\hat{\mathbf{r}}_{12} = (\mathbf{r}_2 -
\mathbf{r}_1)/|\mathbf{r}_2 - \mathbf{r}_1|$.  The two angular coordinates are:
\begin{align}
  \cos\theta_1 &= -\hat{\mathbf{n}}_1 \cdot \hat{\mathbf{r}}_{12} \label{eq:ang1}\\
  \cos\theta_2 &= +\hat{\mathbf{n}}_2 \cdot \hat{\mathbf{r}}_{12} \label{eq:ang2}
\end{align}
Physically, when two lipids face each other head-to-head across the bilayer
midplane, $\cos\theta_1 \approx +1$ and $\cos\theta_2 \approx +1$.

\subsection{CG Lipid -- CG Lipid Quad-Spline Potential}
\label{sec:cg-cg}

The anisotropic interaction between two CG lipid particles is described by a
factorized quad-spline potential that separates the angular-averaged isotropic
core from orientation-dependent modes:

\begin{equation}
\boxed{
U_{\text{CG-CG}}(r, \cos\theta_1, \cos\theta_2)
= V_0(r) + \sum_{m=1}^{M} A^{(1)}_m(\cos\theta_1) \;
                               A^{(2)}_m(\cos\theta_2) \;
                               V_m(r)
}
\label{eq:cg-cg}
\end{equation}

where $M = 4$ modes are retained, and all one-dimensional functions are
represented as cubic B-splines (order $k=4$) with clamped de Boor boundary
conditions~\cite{deBoor}:

\begin{itemize}
  \item $V_0(r)$: isotropic radial core, $N_r = 14$ control points on
    $r \in [0, 16.8]\,\text{\AA}$, uniform knot spacing
    $\Delta r = 1.4\,\text{\AA}$.
  \item $A^{(1,2)}_m(\cos\theta)$: angular modulation profiles for lipid 1 and
    lipid 2, $N_\theta = 15$ control points on $\cos\theta \in [-1, 1]$.
  \item $V_m(r)$: radial envelope of mode $m$, $N_r = 14$ control points.
\end{itemize}

The total number of stored coefficients per CG-CG type pair is
$N_r + M(2N_\theta + N_r) = 14 + 4(30 + 14) = 190$.

\paragraph{Tapering.}
A cubic Hermite taper $S(u) = u^2(3 - 2u)$ with
$u = (r_{\text{cut}} - r) / w$ smoothly drives the potential to zero at the cutoff;
the taper width $w = \Delta r = 1.4\,\text{\AA}$.

\paragraph{Table Construction.}
The potential is constructed from the underlying dry MARTINI force field by
sampling the total nonbonded energy (Lennard-Jones plus Coulomb) between two
explicit 14-bead DOPC lipids at a regular grid of $(r, \cos\theta_1,
\cos\theta_2)$ values and averaging over azimuthal orientations. For each
sampled configuration, the internal bead coordinates are first relaxed via
steepest-descent energy minimization ($N_{\text{relax}} = 50$ steps) to avoid
rigid-body steric clashes. The 3D energy grid
$E(r_i, \cos\theta_{1,j}, \cos\theta_{2,k})$ is capped at
$E_{\text{cap}} = 500\,\text{kJ/mol}$ and decomposed mode-by-mode:

\begin{enumerate}
  \item $V_0(r) = \langle E \rangle_{\cos\theta_1, \cos\theta_2}$ (angular average).
  \item Residual $\Delta(r, \cos\theta_1, \cos\theta_2) = E - V_0$ is
    reshaped to a matrix per radial slice; SVD extracts the dominant left and
    right singular vectors as $A^{(1)}_m$ and $A^{(2)}_m$, with $V_m(r)$ set
    to the corresponding singular value times the right singular vector.
  \item The mode contribution is subtracted and the procedure repeats for
    $M$ modes.
\end{enumerate}

Angular profiles $A^{(1,2)}_m$ are symmetrized (averaged when the two lipids
are identical), normalized to unit maximum absolute value, and fitted with a
cubic B-spline. Radial functions $V_0(r)$ and $V_m(r)$ are fitted with cubic
B-splines using Tikhonov regularization (smoothing parameter $\lambda = 0.01$)
to penalize the second derivative.  Control points at small $r$ where no
training data exist ($r < 7.0\,\text{\AA}$, corresponding to knot indices
$0$--$4$) are clamped to the positive energy cap
$+E_{\text{cap}} / \kappa_E \approx +171.5\,E_{\text{up}}$ to enforce a
repulsive steric barrier.

\subsection{CG Lipid -- Sidechain Quad-Spline Potential}
\label{sec:cg-sc}

The interaction between a CG lipid particle and a protein sidechain (SC) bead
uses the same functional form as Eq.~\ref{eq:cg-cg}, with the SC bead treated
as source~1 ($\cos\theta_1 = -\hat{\mathbf{n}}_{\text{SC}} \cdot
\hat{\mathbf{r}}_{12}$) and the CG lipid as source~2
($\cos\theta_2 = +\hat{\mathbf{n}}_{\text{CG}} \cdot
\hat{\mathbf{r}}_{12}$). The SC direction $\hat{\mathbf{n}}_{\text{SC}}$ is the
unit vector from C$_\alpha$ to C$_\beta$.

The table is built with the same grid sampling, SVD decomposition, and
B-spline fitting procedure, but with $N_{\text{relax}} = 120$ bead relaxation
steps and a tighter angular grid (9 $\cos\theta$ points $\times$ 4 azimuthal
angles). Separate tables are constructed for each of the 18 standard
proteinogenic sidechain types against the single CG lipid type.

\subsection{CG Lipid Orientation Restraint}
\label{sec:cg-spring}

To provide quadratic stiffness against angular drift of the CG lipid
orientation vector, an orientation spring potential is applied:

\begin{equation}
U_{\text{orient}}(\hat{\mathbf{n}}) = k_{\text{orient}} \,
\bigl(1 - \hat{\mathbf{n}} \cdot \hat{\mathbf{n}}_{\text{ref}} \bigr)
\label{eq:orient}
\end{equation}

where $\hat{\mathbf{n}}_{\text{ref}}$ is the initial orientation assigned at
system preparation, and $k_{\text{orient}} = 50.0\,E_{\text{up}}$.
For small angular deviations $\theta$, $1 - \cos\theta \approx \theta^2/2$,
giving an effective harmonic restraint. The single harmonic bond anchoring the
orientation site (Sec.~2.1) provides only quartic stiffness
$\propto \theta^4$; this spring adds the missing quadratic term~\cite{Upside}.

\subsection{CG Lipid -- Environment Isotropic Potential}
\label{sec:cg-iso}

Interactions between CG lipid particles and isotropic environment beads (water,
ions, protein backbone carrier atoms) are described by a pure radial cubic
B-spline with no angular dependence:

\begin{equation}
U_{\text{CG-env}}(r) = B_{\text{spline}}(r; \,\{c_k\}_{k=0}^{N_r-1})
\label{eq:cg-iso}
\end{equation}

where $\{c_k\}$ are $N_r = 14$ control points on the same knot grid as
$V_0(r)$. The B-spline is fitted to an effective Lennard-Jones (12,6) potential
obtained by orientation-averaging the sum of LJ interactions between all 14
DOPC beads and a point probe of the target environment type over a Fibonacci
sphere of 200 directions. The effective LJ parameters $(\varepsilon_{\text{eff}},
\sigma_{\text{eff}})$ are obtained by fitting the orientation-averaged energy.
For the CG-CG isotropic self-term, a physically motivated override is used:
$\sigma = 10.0\,\text{\AA}$, $\varepsilon = 1.0\,E_{\text{up}}$.

\subsection{Hybrid Interface: Sidechain -- Environment Interaction}
\label{sec:sc-env}

Protein sidechain beads interact with the dry MARTINI environment (water and
ion beads) through a factorized orientation-dependent potential:

\begin{equation}
U_{\text{SC-env}}(r, \cos\theta) = V_{\text{rad}}(r)
                                 + A(\cos\theta) \cdot V_{\text{ang}}(r)
\label{eq:sc-env}
\end{equation}

where $r = |\mathbf{r}_{\text{env}} - \mathbf{r}_{\text{CB}}|$ is the distance
from the environment bead to the C$_\beta$ anchor point, and
$\cos\theta = -(\mathbf{r}_{\text{CB}} - \mathbf{r}_{\text{env}}) \cdot
\hat{\mathbf{n}}_{\text{SC}} / r$.

\paragraph{Underlying Physics.}
The tables are precomputed from the dry MARTINI nonbonded potential between a
sidechain bead (placed at rotamer centers) and an environment probe
particle:

\begin{align}
E_{\text{LJ}}(r) &= 4\varepsilon_{ij} \left[
                    \left(\frac{\sigma_{ij}}{r}\right)^{12}
                  - \left(\frac{\sigma_{ij}}{r}\right)^{6} \right] \label{eq:lj} \\
E_{\text{Coul}}(r) &= \frac{k_C}{\epsilon_r} \frac{q_i q_j}{r} \label{eq:coul}
\end{align}

with $k_C = 138.935\,\text{kJ}\cdot\text{nm}\cdot\text{mol}^{-1}\cdot e^{-2}$
and dielectric constant $\epsilon_r = 15$. At each sampled $(r, \cos\theta)$,
the sidechain bead positions from all rotamers are placed; the total energy is
computed as the sum over SC beads of $E_{\text{LJ}}(r_{ij}) +
E_{\text{Coul}}(r_{ij})$, where $r_{ij}$ is the distance from each SC bead at
its rotamer position to the probe point. The 2D energy grid
$E(r_i, \cos\theta_j)$ is factorized via SVD:

\begin{itemize}
  \item $V_{\text{rad}}(r) = \langle E \rangle_{\cos\theta}$
  \item Residual $\Delta(r, \cos\theta) = E - V_{\text{rad}}$, then
    $\Delta = U \cdot S \cdot V^T$; $A(\cos\theta) = U_{:,0}$ (normalized to
    $\max|A| = 1$) and $V_{\text{ang}}(r) = S_{00} \cdot V_{0,:}$.
\end{itemize}

The resulting tables are stored as piecewise-linear grids with 96 radial points
($r \in [2.5, 12.0]\,\text{\AA}$) and 13 angular bins
($\cos\theta \in [-1, 1]$). Rotamer-resolved variants are also available
through a 1-body table used by the sidechain rotamer optimization node.

\paragraph{Interface Scaling.}
The SC-env potential is multiplied by a global interface scale factor
$\alpha_{\text{interface}}$ (default 1.0, calibrated against implicit-membrane
reference simulations). During the hybrid simulation startup phase, SC-env
forces are linearly ramped from zero over $N_{\text{relax}} = 150$ steps with a
force cap of $25.0\,E_{\text{up}}/\text{\AA}$, while backbone degrees of freedom
are held nearly fixed by a backbone feedback mixing protocol over
$N_{\text{hold}} = 200$ steps.

\subsection{Hybrid Interface: Backbone -- Environment Interaction}
\label{sec:bb-env}

Protein backbone carrier atoms (N, C$_\alpha$, C, O) interact with the dry
MARTINI environment through the \textbf{standard Martini potential}, which
evaluates the combined LJ and Coulomb energy via a precomputed cubic B-spline:

\begin{equation}
U_{\text{BB-env}}(r) = B_{\text{spline}}\bigl(r; \; 4\varepsilon(\sigma^{12}r^{-12}
                      - \sigma^6 r^{-6}) + k_C q_i q_j / (\epsilon_r r) \bigr)
\label{eq:bb-env}
\end{equation}

The combined energy $E_{\text{LJ}}(r) + E_{\text{Coul}}(r)$ is pre-tabulated on
a uniform grid of 1000 points per unique $(\varepsilon, \sigma, q_i q_j)$
triple and fitted with a layered clamped B-spline~\cite{Upside}. Forces on
backbone proxy atoms are projected onto the underlying Upside backbone degrees
of freedom. Cross-interface BB--env pairs are scaled by
$\alpha_{\text{interface}}$.

\subsection{Martini Potential Zeroing Protocol}

To prevent double-counting, all entries in the standard Martini pair-parameter
table involving the CG lipid type (``CGL'') are explicitly set to zero
$(\varepsilon = 0, \sigma = 0)$ after the dedicated spline tables are built:

\begin{itemize}
  \item CGL--CGL: handled entirely by $U_{\text{CG-CG}}$ (Sec.~\ref{sec:cg-cg}).
  \item CGL--SC: handled entirely by $U_{\text{CG-SC}}$ (Sec.~\ref{sec:cg-sc}).
  \item CGL--isotropic targets: handled entirely by $U_{\text{CG-env}}$
    (Sec.~\ref{sec:cg-iso}).
  \item CGLD (orientation site): zero nonbonded interactions with all types.
\end{itemize}

At runtime, the Martini potential's pair-list construction skips any pair with
$\varepsilon = \sigma = q_i = q_j = 0$, so zeroed entries contribute no energy.

\subsection{Energy Units}

All potential coefficients are stored in upside energy units $E_{\text{up}}$,
where $1\,E_{\text{up}} = 2.914952774272\,\text{kJ/mol}$. Lengths are in
\r{A}ngstr\"{o}ms ($1\,\text{nm} = 10\,\text{\AA}$). The conversion is applied
at table-building time (Python) and at load time (C++), with tail corrections
subtracted so that all potentials decay smoothly to zero at their respective
cutoffs.
